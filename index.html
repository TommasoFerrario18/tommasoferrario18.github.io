Publications

Dynamic User Profiling for Personalized Tourism Recommendations Using Reinforcement Learning Models
Advanced Information Networking and Applications 18 April 2025
Tommaso Ferrario Gabriele Sormani Enza Messina Elisabetta Fersini

This paper addresses the challenges of providing personalized tourism recommendations amidst the vast amount of information available through digital platforms. It proposes two reinforcement learning (RL) models for dynamic user profiling: a Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning (HRL) model with Matrix Factorization and a Double Deep Q-Network (DDQN) model. These models aim to improve recommendations of Points of Interest (POIs) by adapting user profiles based on changing interests in Topics of Interest (TOIs). Experimental results show that the HRL model works well for users with extensive interaction histories, while the DDQN model is better suited for new or less active users. The study emphasizes the potential of RL models for dynamic user profiling and personalized tourism recommendations.
